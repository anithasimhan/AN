Project Overview

What is IDM?

IDM (Identity and Access Management) is a role-based enterprise system used by teams at Northern Trust to securely review, approve, and revoke user access across applications and entitlements. It brings certification workflows, entitlement tracking, and role-based control into one platform.

🔴 The Problem

The IDM experience was deeply confusing. Screens were inconsistent, information was unorganized and overwhelming, and workflows were hard to find. Users had no clear guidance or feedback after key actions — making everyday tasks frustrating and slow.

🎯 The Goal

Redesign the IDM platform to fix usability issues — making screens consistent, reducing information overload, improving navigation structure, and adding clear guidance and confirmations for every key action users take.

Key Pain Points Discovered

🗺️

Hard to Navigate

Users couldn't easily find key workflows and were forced to click through multiple screens just to reach their starting point. There was no clear entry path.

🔀

Inconsistent Screens

Pages looked and behaved differently from each other, making the system feel unpredictable. Users couldn't build confidence using the product.

📊

Information Overload

Too much data appeared at once without clear priority or hierarchy. Users didn't know what to focus on, and important information was buried.

❓

Lack of Guidance & Feedback

After completing an action, users had no confirmation it worked. There was no next step guidance, leaving users unsure whether to continue or stop.

SWOT Analysis

Evaluating the Product Honestly

Before moving into ideation, I conducted a SWOT analysis on the existing IDM system. This helped the team understand what was already working, what was broken, where the real opportunities were, and what risks needed to be addressed in the redesign.

💪 Strengths

  • Role-based access control already in place — strong security foundation
  • Certification and verification workflows existed — just poorly designed
  • System used by a large, consistent user base daily
  • Entitlement tracking covered across applications and end elements

⚠️ Weaknesses

  • Screens were inconsistent — each page looked and behaved differently
  • Information overload — too much data displayed with no clear priority
  • Navigation was hard — users could not find key workflows easily
  • No clear guidance or feedback after completing key actions
  • Bulk review flows were confusing and time-consuming

🚀 Opportunities

  • Introduce a clean dashboard with KPIs and quick access to pending tasks
  • Restructure navigation around how users actually work — Certification, Verification, Rule Management
  • Add sticky action bars and collapsible sections to reduce cognitive load
  • Introduce progress tracking and clear confirmation states after every action
  • Improve bulk review experience with smarter filtering and grouping

🔻 Threats

  • Poor navigation could cause users to make access approval errors
  • Information overload risks incorrect decisions during bulk reviews
  • Existing bad patterns may be hard to change due to user habits
  • Design system constraints limit new UI elements without library team approval
"The SWOT analysis was a turning point — it shifted the team's thinking from 'what looks broken' to 'what is the actual risk to users and the business.' That change in perspective made every design decision sharper."

Test

Usability Testing & Validation

After completing the redesign, I validated the solution with 15 real end users to evaluate whether the new designs actually solved the problems we identified. Sessions were moderated one-on-one, mirroring the same structured approach used in the initial research.

📊 Testing Approach

15 end users tested the redesigned interface across all major workflows. We measured task completion rate, time on task, error rate, and navigation accuracy — and gathered qualitative feedback on clarity and confidence.

✅ Success Criteria

We defined success as users completing their primary tasks without confusion, errors, or needing assistance — within a significantly shorter time than the original system.

What Users Said & Did

🧭

Faster Navigation

Users could now locate key workflows — certifications, approvals, and access reviews — significantly faster than before. No more clicking through multiple unrelated screens.

🎨

Consistent Experience

The consistent layouts across all screens made navigation feel intuitive and predictable. Users built confidence quickly and didn't need to re-learn patterns on each page.

📉

Reduced Information Overload

The simplified structure and prioritised information hierarchy significantly reduced the overwhelming feeling users had with the original interface.

💬

Clearer Guidance

Clearer labels, confirmation messages, and next-step prompts helped users understand exactly what to do after every action — eliminating the confusion and second-guessing.

⚡

Fewer Steps, Less Confusion

Overall, tasks were completed with fewer steps and significantly less confusion. Users described the new experience as feeling "guided" and "much cleaner."

👍

Positive User Sentiment

All 15 users gave positive feedback. The redesign was described as a major improvement — with multiple users noting they felt "more in control" of the system.

🎉

A Big Win for Users, the Team & the Client

The IDM redesign proved that deep user research, structured collaboration, and a consistent design system can transform even the most complex enterprise workflows into clear, confident experiences.

Key Learnings

What I Took Away

🔬 Research First, Always

Every major design decision in this project was backed by something a real user said or did. That discipline saved us from designing based on assumptions and kept the solution grounded.

🤝 Collaboration Unlocks Better Ideas

The brainstorming sessions with developers and the project manager surfaced constraints and opportunities I wouldn't have found designing alone. Cross-functional input made every solution stronger.

📐 Design Systems Build Trust

Using Northern Trust's existing design system wasn't a constraint — it was a superpower. Consistency across every screen built user confidence and made developer handoff significantly smoother.
